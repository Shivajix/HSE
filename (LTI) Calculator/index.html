Key LTI Metrics Explained

LTI Frequency Rate (LTIFR)

LTIFR = (Number of LTIs × 1,000,000) / Employee Hours Worked

LTI Frequency Rate measures the number of lost time injuries per 1,000,000 hours worked. It indicates how frequently serious injuries occur in your workplace.

LTI Severity Rate (LTISR)

LTISR = (Total Lost Workdays × 1,000,000) / Employee Hours Worked

LTI Severity Rate measures the number of lost workdays per 1,000,000 hours worked. It indicates the severity and impact of lost time injuries.

LTI Incidence Rate (LTIIR)

LTIIR = (Number of LTIs × 200,000) / Employee Hours Worked

LTI Incidence Rate measures the number of lost time injuries per 100 full-time employees. It provides a standardized measure for comparison across organizations.

LTI Classification & Categories

Days Away Cases

Injuries that result in days away from work beyond the day of injury. The employee is unable to perform any work duties.

Restricted Work Cases

Injuries where the employee can work but with restrictions on duties, hours, or work location as recommended by healthcare provider.

Job Transfer Cases

Injuries that require permanent or temporary transfer to another job as a result of work-related injury or illness.

Medical Treatment Cases

Injuries requiring significant medical treatment beyond first aid that result in work restrictions or time away from work.

How to Calculate Lost Time Injury Rates

1

Identify Lost Time Injuries

Gather data on all lost time injuries that occurred during the measurement period. An LTI is any work-related injury or illness that results in an employee being unable to work their next regularly scheduled workday or shift.

2

Calculate Total Employee Hours

Calculate the total number of hours worked by all employees during the same measurement period. Include all hours worked - regular time, overtime, but exclude vacation, sick leave, holidays, and other non-work hours.

3

Apply the LTI Formula

Use the appropriate formula based on the metric you need: LTIFR = (Number of LTIs × 1,000,000) / Total Hours or LTIIR = (Number of LTIs × 200,000) / Total Hours.

4

Interpret and Take Action

Compare your calculated LTI rates with previous periods, organizational targets, and industry benchmarks. Use the insights to implement targeted safety improvements.
