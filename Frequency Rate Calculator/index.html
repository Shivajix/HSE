Key Safety Metrics Explained

Frequency Rate (FR)

FR = (Number of Lost Time Injuries × 1,000,000) / Employee Hours Worked

Frequency Rate measures the number of lost time injuries per 1,000,000 hours worked. It indicates how often injuries resulting in time away from work occur in your organization.

Severity Rate (SR)

SR = (Total Lost Days × 1,000,000) / Employee Hours Worked

Severity Rate measures the number of lost days per 1,000,000 hours worked. It indicates the severity of injuries by measuring the impact on work time.

Incident Rate (IR)

IR = (Number of Recordable Incidents × 200,000) / Employee Hours Worked

Incident Rate measures all recordable incidents (including minor injuries) per 100 full-time employees. Provides a broader view of safety performance.

How to Calculate Frequency Rate

1

Gather Lost Time Injury Data

Collect accurate data on all lost time injuries that occurred during the measurement period. A lost time injury is any work-related injury that results in an employee being unable to work their next scheduled shift.

2

Calculate Total Employee Hours

Calculate the total number of hours worked by all employees during the same measurement period. Include regular hours, overtime, but exclude vacation, sick leave, and other non-work hours.

3

Apply the Frequency Rate Formula

Use the formula: FR = (Number of Lost Time Injuries × 1,000,000) / Total Employee Hours Worked. The 1,000,000 factor standardizes the rate to represent injuries per million hours worked.

4

Interpret and Analyze Results

Compare your calculated FR with previous periods, organizational targets, and industry benchmarks. Lower FR values indicate better safety performance.
