Essential Electrical Safety Tips

Proper Grounding

Ensure all electrical equipment is properly grounded. Use GFCI outlets in wet or damp locations to prevent electrical shocks.

Lockout/Tagout

Always follow LOTO procedures when working on electrical equipment. Verify de-energization before starting work.

PPE Requirements

Wear appropriate personal protective equipment including insulated gloves, face shields, and flame-resistant clothing.

Clear Working Space

Maintain clear working space around electrical panels and equipment as required by electrical codes and standards.

Qualified Personnel Only

Only qualified and trained personnel should perform electrical work. Never attempt electrical repairs without proper training.

Electrical Fire Safety

Use Class C fire extinguishers for electrical fires. Never use water on electrical fires as it can cause electrocution.

Common Electrical Hazards

Electric Shock

Contact with energized electrical circuits can cause severe injury or electrocution. Always verify circuits are de-energized.

Arc Flash

Sudden release of electrical energy through air can cause severe burns, hearing damage, and blast injuries.

Electrical Fires

Overloaded circuits, faulty wiring, or equipment malfunctions can lead to electrical fires and property damage.

Inadequate Grounding

Improper grounding can lead to equipment damage, electrical shocks, and increased risk of electrical fires.

Wet Conditions

Water and electricity create extreme shock hazards. Use appropriate equipment and procedures in damp locations.

Overloaded Circuits

Exceeding circuit capacity can cause overheating, equipment damage, and create fire hazards.
