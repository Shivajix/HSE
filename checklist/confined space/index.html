Common Confined Space Types

Tanks & Vessels

Storage tanks, reactors, pressure vessels, and other enclosed containers that may contain hazardous atmospheres or materials.

Pipes & Ductwork

Underground pipes, large diameter pipelines, ventilation ducts, and other tunnel-like confined spaces with limited access.

Excavations & Pits

Manholes, utility vaults, sumps, pits, and excavations deeper than 4 feet that may accumulate hazardous gases.

Silos & Hoppers

Grain silos, material hoppers, bins, and other storage structures with potential for engulfment or atmospheric hazards.

Vessel Interiors

Ship voids, barge compartments, ballast tanks, and other marine confined spaces with complex access challenges.

Process Equipment

Furnaces, boilers, degreasers, mixing vessels, and other industrial equipment requiring internal inspection or maintenance.

Essential Confined Space Safety Requirements

Atmospheric Testing

Test for oxygen levels, flammable gases, and toxic substances before and during entry using calibrated equipment.

Ventilation

Provide adequate ventilation to maintain safe atmospheric conditions throughout the entry operation.

Entry Permit System

Implement a comprehensive permit system documenting hazards, controls, and authorized personnel.

Communication

Maintain constant communication between entrants and attendants using reliable communication systems.

Rescue Planning

Develop and practice emergency rescue procedures with properly trained personnel and equipment.

Personal Protective Equipment

Provide appropriate PPE including retrieval systems, respiratory protection, and protective clothing.
