Key Safety Severity Metrics

Severity Rate (SR)

SR = (Total Lost Workdays × 1,000,000) / Employee Hours Worked

Severity Rate measures the number of lost workdays per 1,000,000 hours worked. It indicates the severity of workplace injuries by quantifying the impact on work time and productivity.

Average Severity Rate

Avg. Severity = Total Lost Workdays / Number of Lost Time Injuries

Average Severity measures the average number of lost workdays per lost time injury. It helps understand the typical impact of injuries when they do occur.

Frequency-Severity Index

FSI = √(Frequency Rate × Severity Rate)

FSI combines both frequency and severity into a single metric, providing a comprehensive view of safety performance that accounts for both how often and how severe injuries are.

How to Calculate Severity Rate

1

Gather Lost Workday Data

Collect accurate data on all lost workdays resulting from workplace injuries during the measurement period. Include calendar days employees were unable to work, excluding the day of injury but including weekends and holidays.

2

Calculate Total Employee Hours

Calculate the total number of hours worked by all employees during the same measurement period. Include regular hours, overtime, but exclude vacation, sick leave (unless work-related), and other non-work hours.

3

Apply the Severity Rate Formula

Use the formula: SR = (Total Lost Workdays × 1,000,000) / Total Employee Hours Worked. The 1,000,000 factor standardizes the rate to represent lost days per million hours worked.

4

Interpret and Benchmark Results

Compare your calculated SR with previous periods, organizational targets, and industry benchmarks. Lower SR values indicate better control over injury severity and impact.

Severity Rate Classification

Low Severity

Minor injuries with minimal lost time. Typically involves first aid cases or injuries requiring limited medical treatment with quick return to work.

SR < 50

Moderate Severity

Injuries resulting in significant lost time but full recovery expected. Requires medical treatment and modified duties during recovery.

SR 50 - 200

High Severity

Serious injuries with extended lost time, potential for permanent partial disability, or significant medical costs.

SR 200 - 500

Critical Severity

Catastrophic injuries including fatalities, permanent total disabilities, or injuries requiring extensive medical treatment and rehabilitation.

SR > 500
