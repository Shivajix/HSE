What Are Safe Man Hours?

Safe Man Hours represent the total number of working hours completed by employees without any incident that results in lost time due to injury (LTI - Lost Time Injury).

Key Concept: If an LTI occurs, the safe man hours counter resets to zero, and counting begins again from that point. This creates a powerful incentive for maintaining safe working conditions.

This metric is crucial for measuring safety performance in industries like construction, manufacturing, oil and gas, and other high-risk environments.

Lost Time Injury (LTI)

Any work-related injury that results in an employee being unable to perform their regular job duties for one or more full days after the incident.

Safe Hours Accumulation

The continuous counting of work hours without any LTI incidents, representing the organization's safety performance.

Reset Mechanism

When an LTI occurs, the safe man hours counter resets to zero, emphasizing the importance of consistent safety practices.

Formula for Safe Man Hours

The calculation for safe man hours is straightforward but requires accurate tracking of workforce data.

Basic Safe Man Hours Formula

Safe Man Hours = Σ(Number of Workers × Hours Worked per Day × Days Worked)

Where Σ represents the sum across all workforce categories and time periods since the last LTI.

For more precise calculations that account for different worker categories and overtime:

Detailed Calculation Formula

Total Hours = (Regular Hours + Overtime Hours) × Number of Workers × Days Worked

It's important to track hours for all personnel on site, including contractors and temporary workers, for a comprehensive safety measurement.

FAQ

What counts as a Lost Time Injury (LTI)?

An LTI is any work-related injury that results in an employee being unable to perform their regular job duties for one or more full days after the incident. This includes injuries that require medical treatment beyond first aid.

How should we handle contractor hours in our calculations?

Contractor hours should be included in your safe man hours calculations. All personnel working on your site, regardless of employment status, should be tracked for comprehensive safety measurement and to ensure everyone's safety is prioritized.

Can we include hours from multiple sites in our safe man hours?

Yes, but it's important to track them separately as well as collectively. This allows you to identify specific sites that may need additional safety focus while still presenting an overall organizational safety performance.

How often should we reset our safe man hours counter?

The counter should only reset when an LTI occurs. Some organizations also reset annually for reporting purposes, but the primary counter should reflect continuous hours since the last LTI to maintain accuracy in safety performance measurement.

What's the difference between safe man hours and man hours?

Man hours refer to the total hours worked by all employees, while safe man hours specifically track hours worked without any lost time injuries. Safe man hours reset after an LTI, emphasizing the importance of continuous safety.
