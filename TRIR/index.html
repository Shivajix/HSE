Key Safety Metrics Explained

TRIR (Total Recordable Incident Rate)

TRIR = (Number of Recordable Injuries × 200,000) / Employee Hours Worked

TRIR measures the number of recordable injuries per 100 full-time employees during a one-year period. It's the most commonly used safety metric in industry for overall safety performance.

AFR (Average Frequency Rate)

AFR = (Number of Lost Time Injuries × 1,000,000) / Employee Hours Worked

AFR measures the frequency of lost time injuries per 1,000,000 hours worked. It focuses specifically on injuries that result in time away from work.

FAR (Fatal Accident Rate)

FAR = (Number of Fatalities × 100,000,000) / Employee Hours Worked

FAR measures the number of fatalities per 100 million hours worked. This metric is particularly important in high-risk industries and for catastrophic event prevention.

How to Calculate Safety Metrics

1

Gather Incident Data

Collect accurate data on all recordable incidents, lost time injuries, and fatalities that occurred during the measurement period. Ensure proper classification according to OSHA standards and your organization's safety protocols.

2

Calculate Total Employee Hours

Calculate the total number of hours worked by all employees during the same measurement period. Include regular hours, overtime, but exclude vacation, sick leave, holidays, and other non-work hours.

3

Apply the Appropriate Formula

Use the correct formula for each metric: TRIR uses 200,000 as the base, AFR uses 1,000,000, and FAR uses 100,000,000. These bases standardize the rates for comparison across organizations.

4

Interpret and Benchmark Results

Compare your calculated metrics with previous periods, organizational targets, and industry benchmarks. Use these insights to drive safety improvement initiatives and measure their effectiveness.

Industry Benchmark Standards

Construction Industry

Typically has higher rates due to inherent risks. Focus on reducing falls, struck-by, and electrocution hazards.

TRIR: 3.0-4.0

Manufacturing

Moderate rates with focus on machine guarding, ergonomics, and chemical safety improvements.

TRIR: 2.0-3.0

Maintenance & Repair

Varied rates depending on industry served. Strong focus on LOTO procedures and confined space safety.

TRIR: 2.5-3.5

Warehousing & Logistics

Focus on material handling safety, vehicle operations, and slip/trip/fall prevention measures.

TRIR: 3.5-4.5
