Severity Rate Calculator - Complete Guide

Measure injury severity and lost work days for safety performance

What is Severity Rate?

The Severity Rate Calculator measures the average number of days lost per recordable injury, providing insight into the seriousness of workplace injuries and their impact on operations.

Key Metric

Severity Rate represents the average severity of injuries in terms of lost work days, complementing Frequency Rate to provide a complete safety performance picture.

Calculation Methods

There are two primary standards for calculating Severity Rate:

OSHA Method (USA Standard)

Per 200,000 hours worked

SR = (Total Lost Work Days × 200,000) / Total Hours Worked

International Method (ISO Standard)

Per 1,000,000 hours worked

SR = (Total Lost Work Days × 1,000,000) / Total Hours Worked

Formula Components

Lost Work Days

Days away from work due to work-related injury or illness, including:

  • Calendar days employees are unable to work
  • Days of restricted work or job transfer
  • Permanent disabilities or fatalities

Total Hours Worked

The sum of all hours worked by all employees during the period being measured.

How to Use the Severity Rate Calculator

  1. Select calculation method - OSHA (200,000 hours) or International (1,000,000 hours)
  2. Enter total lost work days during the measurement period
  3. Enter total hours worked by all employees during the same period
  4. The calculator automatically computes the severity rate
  5. Analyze results to identify severity trends and areas for improvement

Our free calculator allows you to calculate using both OSHA (US standard) and International (ISO) methods for comprehensive safety performance analysis.

Example Calculations

Example 1 - Construction Project:
  • Total Lost Work Days: 45
  • Total Hours Worked: 250,000
  • OSHA Method: SR = (45 × 200,000) / 250,000 = 36.0
  • International Method: SR = (45 × 1,000,000) / 250,000 = 180.0
Example 2 - Manufacturing Plant:
  • Total Lost Work Days: 12
  • Total Hours Worked: 180,000
  • OSHA Method: SR = (12 × 200,000) / 180,000 = 13.3
  • International Method: SR = (12 × 1,000,000) / 180,000 = 66.7
Example 3 - Oil & Gas Operation:
  • Total Lost Work Days: 8
  • Total Hours Worked: 120,000
  • OSHA Method: SR = (8 × 200,000) / 120,000 = 13.3
  • International Method: SR = (8 × 1,000,000) / 120,000 = 66.7

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between Severity Rate and Frequency Rate? +

Frequency Rate measures how often injuries occur, while Severity Rate measures how serious they are in terms of lost work days. Both metrics are needed for a complete safety performance picture.

How are restricted work days counted in Severity Rate? +

Restricted work days are counted as full lost work days in Severity Rate calculations. If an employee can't perform their regular job duties but can do restricted work, those days are included in the total.

Which calculation method should I use - OSHA or International? +

Use the OSHA method if you're in the United States or need to comply with US regulations. Use the International method for global operations or ISO compliance. Be consistent with your chosen method for accurate trend analysis.
